Top takeaways

1

Core functionality is powerful and intuitive

As a marketer with no customer support or support engineering experience, I was pretty easily able to get the default layer working and see the core value. The product is easy to navigate, the workflows are intuitive, and the AI agents were quick to set up and were producing helpful responses almost immediately. I know the real unlock is the API and extensibility — I wasn't able to experience that directly, but I'm curious about your take on the trial's role in selling that value.

2

There's opportunity to improve the trial / first-run experience to drive conversion

I'm coming to the trial from a growth perspective, and saw levers to pull to improve conversion to a paid plan. Some ideas: a richer landing page with value-prop framed quick actions (e.g. "Bring all your support channels into one inbox" with a tile for each channel), an optional multi-step product tour on core concepts, and gamification for milestones like first channel connected and first thread resolved. Since self-serve already drives revenue with little investment, the trial experience is a high-leverage place to focus.

3

AI is central to the product, and the pieces I tried were promising

In my testing I saw the power of the agents, and also hit a few moments where the AI acted differently than expected (like applying a label outside the conditions I set). I can't say how representative that is from a short session and being a new user, but it made me curious how much visibility and control users have when AI makes a decision — which feels like it matters for a technical ICP that likes to stay in control.

  • Builder is simple, intuitive, and fast to launch automations

  • Test / dry-run that simulates a workflow against an existing thread and shows what each step would do, without making the changes
  • Step-by-step run history to troubleshoot
  • NLP workflows: describe it in plain English, Plain builds it with the code exposed
Plain AI
  • Auto-triages and labels threads automatically, no manual tagging
  • AI applied a label even though the thread didn't meet my conditions, and kept doing it after I added AI guidance, with no visibility into why
  • Transparency and override when AI acts, e.g. a callout like "Plain AI can add this label, change in settings," with an easy way to turn it off
Sidekick
  • Easy to reach from a thread
  • Responses are helpful
  • Didn't suggest the obvious Agent-handoff filter for my problem
  • Eventually broke and wouldn't stop "Thinking"
  • Make Sidekick accessible everywhere in the product, not just threads
Ari
  • Fast, easy setup
  • Added a sitemap and had it responding to threads quickly
  • A log of all Ari changes and responses lives in the thread, making it easy to track
  • Responses were detailed and seemed accurate and helpful
  • After handing off, Ari unassigned itself and I couldn't figure out how to reassign the thread to myself when the agent status was "handed off"
  • Clearer handoff controls: make it obvious how to take a thread back from Ari and assign it to a person after handoff
Tasks
  • A low-friction, lightweight to-do
  • Limited functionality (can't link a Jira ticket or comment on a task), so it's unclear whether Tasks replaces or integrates with existing issue trackers, or is just for individual use
  • Clarify its positioning versus issue trackers, or lean into it as a personal to-do
  • Add linking and commenting if it's meant for escalation
Integrations
  • Create and link a Jira issue directly from a thread
  • Discoverability: I forgot I could create a Jira ticket from the thread
  • Native issue-tracker action in Workflows (today it needs an HTTP / API call)
  • Nudges to surface the capability
Insights
  • Lots of great charts and KPIs tracked in Reporting
 Nothing major
  • Add agent-impact views like % of issues resolved by agents, to tie support data to KPI outcomes
Onboarding / first-run
  • I was able to set up the core functionality as a non-support and non-engineer user
  • Landing / first-run page isn't very rich
  • Light on product tips and guidance
  • Richer first-run page with value-prop framed quick actions
  • Optional multi-step product tour on core concepts
  • Gamification for goalposts like first channel and first resolved thread
  • Tooltips and nudges
Pricing
  • I was able to see when my trial ends
  • On the trial pricing page, it's not clear that the Horizon plan includes 3 seats for $269
  • For the Horizon plan, reframe as price per seat for a lower sticker price (if that's the right lingo)
  • Add color / visual hierarchy to steer toward the target plan
  • Tease gated paid-plan features (e.g. Discord channels, Cursor lookup) to surface an upgrade at a moment of realized value

Smaller items & bugs

  • Slack "Ingestion mode" is phrased like it sets conditions ("Decide whether...") but it's a fixed mode picker, not condition-based
  • Workflows: clicking "Delete" gives no feedback, though the workflow is deleted when you return to the list
  • Landing-page setup: the "add an integration" step said "add Linear, Jira, etc.," but when I clicked it (intending to add Jira) it only showed Linear — finding Jira took a moment
